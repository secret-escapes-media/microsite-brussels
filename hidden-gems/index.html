Something off the beaten track

The Musical Instrument Museum

Located in central Brussels, the Musical Instrument Museum is part of the Royal Museum for Art and History. Renowned for its collection of over 1,500 instruments. The museum showcases the instruments and provides visitors with free infrared headphones to listen to almost 200 musical extracts of the instruments on display.

Comic Strip Route

Inspired by the rich history of Franco-Belgian comics centering around the city of Brussels, the collection of huge murals spread throughout the city, known collectively as the Comic Strip Route, bring the area to cartoon life some of the medium's most famous characters.

La Patinoire Royale

La Patinoire Royale welcomes temporary exhibitions exploring the great milestones of artistic creation in Europe during the second half of the 20th century in the fields of plastic arts and design. With a combined surface of about 3,000 m², La Patinoire Royale and Galerie Valérie Bach are one of the largest privately held spaces devoted to modern and contemporary art in Europe.
