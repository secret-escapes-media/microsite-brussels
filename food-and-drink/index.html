Belgium definitely has a taste for extraordinary food

Best known for its beers, waffles, chocolate, speculoos and Brussels sprouts and cheese. The Kingdom of Belgium certainly has style and taste when it comes to culinary art and cuisine. Savour the regional cusine and gourmet traditions.

The most exquisite dishes are masterfully concocted by real wizards, who also happen to be world-famous chef artists. There's more to the success of Belgian chefs than just mussels and chips, steak américain (raw minced meat) and café liégeois (cold dessert with coffee and ice-cream). What they have in common, besides their creativity, is the excellent quality of their products: fresh local produce combined with exceptional beers and soft cheeses.

Bon Bon - Salon De L'artisan Cuisinier

Christophe Hardiquest has managed, by dint of unsung work and self-sacrifice, to make his Bon-Bon a flagship establishment for the entire Belgian scene. He works every day in his kitchen in full view of the dining room on an unending quest for sublime taste perfection.

Taverne du Passage

An old-fashioned portrait of Brussels in the delightful Galerie de la Reine with its nostalgic charm and Art Deco features. There are a variety of Belgian delicacies including croquette de crevettes (shrimp) and eels in sorrel sauce.

Delirium Café

Officially 2004 beers, but actually 2500 depending on the season. At the Delirium, conversation naturally revolves around beer, some flambéed, others with a greenish colour or else served in 4-litre glasses. You can also taste no fewer than 500 Dutch gins and delicious pure malt whiskies here.
